About Me

Hi, my name is Josh! I'm a student at UC Berkeley with a love for space, reading, and being outside. Im majoring in Astrophysics and Physics, and I expect to graduate in 2025.
I'm from Mt. Shasta, a small town in Northern California, and I grew up spending lots of time outdoors. Nature is a big part of who I am, and in 2020, after a lot of hard work, I became an Eagle Scout. Boy Scouts taught me a lot about who I want to be in life, and I plan on using the skills I gained to go on to grad school after I get my degree. I plan to get my Ph.D. in Astrophysics and would love to work at NASA or JPL.
Science is a huge part of who I am, and I want to spend the rest of my life learning as much as I can.
Follow me on my journey to becoming an astrophysicist

Research

The research that interests me the most lies in the Astronomy and Physics fields. I want to spend my time at UC Berkeley researching exoplanets, both searching for new ones and learning more about the ones we've already discovered. Learning more about the universe is one of my biggest goals in life, and the idea of looking off into the distance at other planets fascinates me. I am very passionate about Astrophysics because of the way it blends Physics and Astronomy. I plan on doing everything I can to expand mankind's collective knowledge about space and the astral bodies that reside out there.
